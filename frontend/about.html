What is Fake News?
Fake news is deliberately fabricated information designed to deceive, manipulate public opinion, or damage reputations. It exploits human cognitive biases and spreads virally through social media — often faster than corrections can follow.
Attack Vector
Deepfake Threats
Deepfakes use generative AI to create hyper-realistic but fabricated images, videos, and audio. They're used for fraud, blackmail, political manipulation, and identity theft — and are becoming nearly impossible to spot with the naked eye.
Social Engineering
Disinformation Campaigns
State and non-state actors orchestrate coordinated disinformation campaigns across platforms, using bot networks and synthetic media to shift narratives and erode trust in legitimate institutions.

🛡️ How to Spot Fake News Yourself

01
Check the source. Look up who published the story. Unfamiliar domains, misspellings, or ".co" endings are red flags.
02
Verify with multiple sources. If only one outlet is reporting a major story, be skeptical. Cross-reference with established news organizations.
03
Reverse image search. Right-click on images and search Google to see where they originally appeared and in what context.
04
Read beyond the headline. Sensational headlines often don't match the actual article content. Clickbait is a common tactic.
05
Check the date. Old stories are often recirculated out of context to manipulate current events and emotions.
